Presentation Of Benin

Benin is located in West Africa in the tropical zone between the equator and the Tropic of Cancer (between parallels 6°30' and 12°30' north latitude and meridians 1° and 30°40' east longitude).

It is limited:

  • to the north by the Republic of Niger over 277 km with 120 km bounded by the Niger River ;
  • to the northwest by Burkina Faso (for 386 km);
  • to the west by Togo (651 km),
  • to the east by Nigeria (for 809 km);
  • and to the south by the Atlantic Ocean (on 121 km).

From north to south, it extends over 700 km; the width varies from 125 km (along the coast) to 325 km (at the latitude Tanguiéta-Ségbana).

The country is quite flat. There are five (05) natural regions:

  • A coastal strip, low and sandy, limited by lagoons (coconut groves);
  • A central plain, hilly and monotonous, which rises progressively from 200 to 400 m from the south to the north in the vicinity of Nikki and then drops to the Niger valley and the Kandi basin. The Kandi basin in the northeast is a plain drained by the Sota River and its tributaries that flow in very wide valleys;
  • The Atacora chain in the northwest, where the highest point of the country, Mount Aledjo (658m) is located;
  • The vast Gourma plain in the extreme northwest, between Atacora and the border with Burkina Faso and Togo.
  • The humid savannah occupies most of the country. A few patches of primary forest remain in the south and center. Crops, swampy areas and the immense palm grove of Bas-Benin occupy the rest of the territory.

The Climate

Benin has two (02) types of climate:

In the south, an equatorial climate with high humidity. Alternating dry seasons (November to March and mid-July to mid-September) and rainy seasons (April to mid-July and mid-September to October).

In the center and the north, a tropical climate. A dry season from November to April and a rainy season from June to September.

The harmattan, a hot and dry wind from the Sahara, blows over the entire country during the dry season.

In Cotonou, temperatures have a small range: maximums vary from 28 to 32°C and minimums from 23 to 26°C. The annual rainfall in this city is 1245 mm.

The relief of Benin is not very hilly and includes :

  • a coastal region, low and sandy limited by lagoons;
  • a plateau of ferruginous clay;
  • a silico-clay plateau, dotted with some undergrowth;
  • an the northwest, the Atacora massif (800 meters);
  • to the north-east, the plains of Niger, silico-clayey and very fertile.

Hydrography of Benin

Several rivers cross the country.

The Beninese basin of the Niger River which includes the rivers :

  • the Mekrou : 480 km;
  • Alibori: 427 km;
  • la Sota: 254 km;
  • and the Pendjari.

The Volta River Basin which includes:

  • the Pendjari (420 km) ;
  • the Perma.

The Mono-Couffo basin which includes :

  • the Couffo: 190 km long river (170 km of which is in Benin), which flows into the Ahémé lagoon, which itself flows into the Atlantic Ocean;
  • the Mono: with its 500 km, it serves as a border between Benin and Togo on its lower course and then flows into the Atlantic Ocean.

The Ouémé basin: with its 608 km, the Ouémé flows into the Nokoué lagoon and uses the Lagos and Cotonou channels to communicate with the Atlantic Ocean.

There are also several water bodies in the south:

  • Lake Nokoué: 138 km2;
  • Lake Ahémé : 78 Km2;
  • and the lagoon of Porto-Novo: 35 km2.

Vegetation and fauna

Three (03) types of vegetation characterize Benin:

  • the wooded savannah in the northern Sudanese regions ;
  • the savannah in the center with species such as mahogany, Iroko, Samba ;
  • and the forest in South and Middle Benin.

As for the fauna, there are two (02) national parks in the North:

  • Pendjari Park: 275,000 ha;
  • the park "W": 502.000 ha.

There are elephants, buffaloes, hippos, lions, cheetahs, caimans, antelopes, birds, monkeys, reptiles, leopards, insects, etc.
